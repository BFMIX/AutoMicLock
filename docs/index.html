The Problem

macOS automatically switches your microphone input to Bluetooth devices when they connect. Because of Bluetooth bandwidth limits, activating the headset's microphone forces the connection into a low-quality "Hands-Free Profile". This instantly ruins your audio quality, making everything sound muffled and distorted like a bad phone call.

The Solution

Auto MicLock silently monitors your system's audio. If macOS attempts to switch the input to your Bluetooth headset, the app instantly forces the input back to your Mac's superior built-in microphone. By forcing macOS to ignore the headset's microphone, your Bluetooth headphones remain in high-fidelity mode (AAC/SBC).

How it works (under the hood)

Unlike hacky scripts that run infinite loops in the background, Auto MicLock is a 100% native macOS Menu Bar application written in pure Swift.

CoreAudio Event-Driven

It uses native AudioObjectAddPropertyListenerBlock to listen for hardware changes. It only wakes up when macOS tries to change the default input, meaning 0% CPU usage on standby.

No External Dependencies

It does not rely on Automator, SwitchAudioSource, or bash loops. It directly sets the kAudioDeviceTransportTypeBuiltIn device via low-level system APIs.

SMAppService

It uses the modern macOS 13+ background service API to launch silently at login without creating messy LaunchAgents or plist files.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why does macOS switch microphones automatically?

Apple designed macOS to default to the newest connected audio device. While convenient for speakers, it’s highly problematic for Bluetooth headphones because activating their microphone degrades the output audio quality drastically due to Bluetooth profile limitations.

Why doesn't Apple just fix this?

It's a historical frustration. Apple assumes that if you connect a headset, you want to use its microphone. They haven't provided a user-facing toggle to say "Always prefer my Mac's built-in microphone". Auto MicLock exists to provide that missing toggle.

Does this improve audio quality?

Yes. By preventing your Mac from using the Bluetooth microphone, your headphones stay in the high-bandwidth A2DP profile (stereo, high bitrate). You will hear your colleagues and music in full HD, while they hear you clearly through your Mac's internal microphone.

Do I need to keep the app open?

Auto MicLock sits quietly in your macOS Menu Bar. You can enable "Launch at Login" from the menu so it runs automatically in the background every time you start your Mac. You never have to think about it again.

Is it free and open source?

Yes, 100% free and open source. The project is built with native Swift and CoreAudio APIs, meaning zero telemetry, no data collection, and absolutely no impact on your Mac's performance.
